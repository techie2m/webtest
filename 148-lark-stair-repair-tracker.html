Fee correction worth knowing: a three-family is billed under Albany's commercial schedule ("residential structures with 3 or more units"). Alterations up to $5,000 in cost = $400 flat — not the $100 that applies to 1–2 family homes. The $125 misc figure from your BRC call likely reflects the minor 2–4 step repair scope; the full Option 2 scope probably prices at $400. Budget $450 with the sidewalk permit and treat anything less as good news.

Decision

DIY vs. contractor

Do it yourself

$860 – $1,500
  • Time: 3–4 working days of your labor + permit/shopping runs
  • Starts: as soon as the permit issues (~7–10 business days)
  • Lead: RRP practices are on you — owner-landlords on rentals aren't exempt
  • Risk point: the jacking/leveling step; everything after is honest carpentry
  • Best when: BRC grants the extension and the stringers probe sound

RRP-certified contractor

$3,100 – $5,150
  • Time: a few hours coordinating; 2–4 weeks realistic to completion
  • Savings forgone: roughly $2,200–3,700
  • Lead: certified firm carries compliance, documentation, disposal
  • Bonus: a professional eye on the sunken stringer before commitment
  • Best when: the clock is tight, or demo reveals rot beyond shimming
Recommended path — hybrid: file the permit and do the base/leveling work yourself now, while collecting two RRP-certified quotes in parallel. Quotes are free, they're a live fallback if the stringer is worse than it looks, and together with the filed permit and receipts they give BRC every reason to extend the compliance date.
