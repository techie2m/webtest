Strategic Profitability Takeaways

1 — The PRU overhang is idiosyncratic, not sector-wide. Prudential's Japan misconduct/suspension is the single largest company-specific risk in the group — it explains the rare pairing of a top-tier ~5% yield with a negative implied return. The market is pricing execution risk, not a broken franchise.
2 — Capital-return models are winning the momentum trade. Asset-light, buyback-driven names (Primerica, Lincoln National, Globe Life) are commanding the strongest price momentum, while balance-sheet-heavy annuity/spread businesses (Jackson Financial, Prudential) trade at more compressed multiples.
3 — International exposure cuts both ways. Manulife's Asia-led growth is being rewarded with strong momentum, while Aflac's Japan-heavy book is a drag on its growth score — geography alone doesn't determine the outcome, execution within that geography does.
